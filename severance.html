Severance

Playful and mordantly funny, “Severance” is like a Charlie Kaufman-designed nightmare, from the midcentury-menacing set to the way it sketches the innies’ hermetic lives.

Severance is a science-fiction drama series that premiered on Apple TV+ in 2021. The series, created by Dan Erickson, is an adaptation of the novel of the same name by Ling Ma.

The story follows Candace Chen (played by the talented actress, Hong Chau), a young woman working at a publishing company in New York City. As a pandemic spreads and begins to wipe out most of the population, Candace finds herself one of the few survivors in the city. She decides to join a group of other survivors, who are working for a company called "Severance" to package and ship items for wealthy clients who have fled to safer locations.

The series is told in two timelines, with flashbacks to Candace's life before the pandemic, and her present-day struggles to survive in a world that has been completely turned upside down. The series explores themes of isolation, grief, and the pursuit of identity in a world that no longer exists.

Overall, Severance is a thought-provoking and well-crafted series that combines elements of science fiction, drama, and psychological horror. The performances are strong, particularly Hong Chau, who brings a depth and nuance to her portrayal of Candace. The series also benefits from its sleek and stylish production design, which helps to create an immersive and haunting atmosphere.

While the series can be slow-moving at times, it rewards viewers who stick with it by delivering a compelling and emotional story that is both satisfying and thought-provoking. If you're a fan of science-fiction and post-apocalyptic stories, or simply looking for a well-crafted and emotionally resonant drama, then Severance is definitely worth checking out.
